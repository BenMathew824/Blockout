Blockout is a Chrome extension that helps you stay focused while studying by classifying the page you're viewing against a topic you provide, and blocking it if it's off-topic during a focus session. This page explains what data the extension handles, where it goes, and what control you have over it.

What Blockout reads to work at all

While a focus session is active, Blockout reads the hostname and title of your active browser tab. That's it — not the page's full content, not your browsing history, not anything from tabs you haven't navigated to.

Where that data goes

There are two ways a classification request can be made, depending on whether you've provided your own API key:

  • With your own Anthropic API key (no account required): the hostname, title, and study topic are sent directly from your browser to Anthropic's Claude API. Blockout's own infrastructure never receives or stores this data. Your key is stored only in your browser's local extension storage and is never transmitted anywhere except to Anthropic, as part of that same request.
  • Signed in, without your own key: the hostname, title, and study topic are sent to Blockout's backend (a serverless function), which forwards them to Anthropic using Blockout's own key and returns the result. Blockout's backend does not log or store the hostname, title, or topic — it only records a per-day request count against your account, used solely to enforce a daily usage cap.

If you create an account

Creating an account is optional and only unlocks syncing your stats and allowlist across devices via the Blockout website. If you sign in, the following is stored in our database (Supabase), protected so only you can access your own data:

  • Your email address (used only for login)
  • Hostnames you've been blocked on, and how many times (not full URLs, not page titles, not browsing history)
  • Hostnames you've chosen to always allow
  • The calendar dates you started a focus session (used to calculate your study streak — no times, no durations, no topics)

None of this data is sold, shared with advertisers, or used for anything beyond showing it back to you in your dashboard.

Your control over this data

  • Remove allowlist entries or reset your stats anytime from the dashboard
  • Uninstalling the extension deletes everything stored locally in your browser (your API key, study topic, and local stats)
  • To delete your account and all associated data entirely, contact [ADD SUPPORT EMAIL]

Why Blockout asks for the permissions it does

  • Read and change your data on the websites you visit — needed to show the session countdown directly in the browser tab title, and (separately) to redirect a tab to the blocked page when a site is off-topic
  • Tabs — to read the active tab's URL/title for classification
  • Storage — to save your study topic, API key, allowlist, and stats locally
  • Alarms — to track session timing and periodic account sync
  • Notifications — to show a notification when your focus session ends
  • Web navigation — to detect page navigation, including single-page apps like YouTube, so classification runs on the actual page you're viewing
